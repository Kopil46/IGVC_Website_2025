System Architecture

A clean separation between perception, control, and safety: Jetson runs vision and LiDAR, TM4C handles low-level motor control, and STM32 manages the wireless emergency switch and NRF24L01+ link.

High-Level Overview

The vehicle’s brain is the Jetson Orin NX, processing camera streams for lane detection and LiDAR scans for obstacle detection. It sends commands over UART to a TM4C123GH6PM, which drives the Sabertooth motor controller. A dedicated STM32F446RE runs the NRF24L01+ wireless link for the emergency stop system to keep safety fully independent.

  • Jetson Orin NX: vision, LiDAR, lane detection, obstacle tracking.
  • TM4C123GH6PM: Sabertooth control, PWM outputs, encoder feedback.
  • STM32F446RE: wireless emergency switch with NRF24L01+.
  • 26 V battery with 26→19 V (compute) and 26→5 V (logic) converters.
  • RedBoard drives emergency LED light patterns for clear status feedback.

Key Capabilities

The robot is designed to handle IGVC-style courses with mixed lane markings, obstacles, and GPS waypoints. Safety is integrated at every level: hardware E-stop, wireless kill-switch, and controlled emergency braking.

  • Lane detection on the Jetson using camera feeds and computer vision.
  • Obstacle detection from LiDAR, with distance thresholds and avoidance logic.
  • GPS navigation to follow competition waypoints.
  • Wireless emergency stop using NRF24L01+ and STM32.
  • Emergency lights driven by RedBoard to indicate system state.
